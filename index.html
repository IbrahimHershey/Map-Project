Do you know where the 11th president of the United States is from? What about the 30th? The 38th? Most Americans do not know where a president is from.

But it is good to know the history of the United States and a significant part of that history is where U.S. presidents were born.

Most presidents are from the Northeast or Midwest region of the United States and I wanted to know why.

I talked to Harold Holzer who serves as The Jonathan F. Fanton Director of Hunter College's Roosevelt House Public Policy Institute and is a scholar of Abraham Lincoln and the political culture of the American Civil War Era.

“California, Washington, and Oregon joined the Union (During the American Civil War, the United States of America was referred to as the Union) later than Virginia, New York, and the middle west states,” said Holzer.

Let’s take a look in more detail of where every single United States president is from.
